1  Why Re-legislate At All

India's direct tax framework underwent its biggest structural overhaul in six decades with the Income-tax Act, 2025 (ITA 2025), effective 1 April 2026, replacing the Income-tax Act, 1961 (ITA 1961).

Over 65 years, ITA 1961 gathered more than 4,000 amendments across 65 Finance Acts, leaving a fragmented mess of provisos and cross-references.

The Income Tax Department's own goal: a simplified, modern code with reduced compliance burden — and explicitly, no new tax.

This is the thesis of the whole assignment: ITA 2025 re-codifies the same policy in clearer form. It does not change the tax burden.

2  Structural Comparison
FeatureITA 1961ITA 2025
Sections800+ (with 80C, 115BAC style suffixes)536, plain sequential numbers
Chapters/SchedulesFragmented over decades23 chapters, 16 schedules
Year conceptPrevious Year + Assessment YearSingle "Tax Year"
TDS provisionsScattered, 40+ sections (192–194T)Consolidated, Section 393

Section count fell, but numbering can look bigger in places — ITA 2025 drops alphabet suffixes (80C, 115BAC) for plain sequential numbers. Presentation change, not an expansion of law.

3  "Tax Year" Replaces Two Clocks

Previous Year (income earned) + Assessment Year (income assessed) → merged into one unified Tax Year: 1 April to 31 March.

Nothing changes mechanically — income is still assessed after the year ends. Section 536(3) confirms "tax year" reads as "previous year" for transition purposes.

6  What Has NOT Changed
  • Tax slabs & rates (both regimes) — as per Finance Act, 2025.
  • Sec. 87A rebate — nil tax up to Rs.12 lakh under new regime, retained.
  • Standard deduction of Rs.75,000 for salaried taxpayers continues.
  • 80C / 80D-style deductions still available, renumbered only.
  • New regime stays default; old regime optional via Form 10-IEA.
7  Who Actually Feels This

Individual taxpayers: minimal change now. FY 2025-26 returns still cite 80C/80D/115BAC. Biggest visible shift ahead is wording — "Tax Year" replacing "Assessment Year."

Tax professionals & CAs: the real burden. Must update section tables, ERP/Tally/Zoho TDS codes, templates and case systems — without cross-citing the wrong Act.

Litigation: ITAT, High Courts & Supreme Court run a dual-track system for years, hearing ITA 1961 and ITA 2025 matters side by side.

Policy: mirrors a global shift to simpler, principle-based codes — alongside India's digital tax push (pre-filled returns, AIS, faceless assessment).

8  Critical Analysis
  • Renumbering without policy change still costs real retraining time and money.
  • A multi-year dual-citation window raises the risk of accidental misciting.
  • Since this is re-codification (not reform), old disputes may resurface under new numbers.
  • Success depends on CBDT/judiciary keeping section-mapping guidance current.
9  Conclusion

ITA 2025 modernises the shell — a unified Tax Year, consolidated TDS, tabular presentation and full renumbering — without touching the substantive tax burden.

Section 536 keeps both Acts alive together for years: ITA 1961 for pre-2026 matters, ITA 2025 for everything after.

The section numbers changed. The logic behind them did not.
